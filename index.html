Core principles

No dogma.
Just good decisions.

Frameworks are useful. Principles are durable. Use these to question assumptions and choose what fits the situation in front of you.

01

Context over convention

Treat every “best practice” as a hypothesis, not a rule.

02

Outcomes over output

Measure whether the work helps, not simply whether it shipped.

03

Evidence over opinion

Make uncertainty visible and learn through small experiments.

04

Evolution over perfection

Choose the smallest sound step and adapt as reality changes.

A simple loop

Understand.
Decide.
Learn.

CDD is not another heavyweight methodology. It is a repeatable way to bring the right information into each technical decision.

  1. 01

    Frame the situation

    Name the user need, business goal, technical landscape, constraints, and unknowns. Be specific about what is true now.

  2. 02

    Expose the trade-offs

    Compare viable options against the context. Record what each choice optimises, costs, and makes harder to change.

  3. 03

    Make a bounded decision

    Choose a direction for the current horizon—not forever. Set guardrails and define what would trigger a revisit.

  4. 04

    Close the learning loop

    Observe the outcome, share what changed, and feed the evidence into the next decision.

Start small

Bring context to your next decision.

Pick one upcoming technical choice. Write down the problem, constraints, evidence, and trade-offs before discussing solutions.
